# Spice Android Terms

These terms are written for the private sideload 1.0.0 Android build. The build is not a Play Store release, is not endorsed by any media provider, and is intended for power users who understand that native resolvers can break when providers change.

This file is a practical project notice, not legal advice.

## User Responsibilities

- Use media providers only through your own rights, accounts, region access, and provider rules.
- Do not use Spice to bypass DRM, access controls, paywalls, bot checks, or copyright restrictions.
- Do not use Spice for abusive automation, credential sharing, resale, public rebroadcasting, or bulk scraping.
- Do not upload, share, or distribute content unless you have permission to do so.

## Downloads

Downloads are explicit user actions. They are intended for personal/offline use where the user has permission to keep a copy. The app does not grant rights to third-party content and does not promise that a provider permits every download path exposed by a resolver.

## Shared Playlists

Shared playlists expose playlist names, member identity, and playlist edits to invited members. Invite only people you trust. Owners and editors are responsible for the content they add.

## Provider Availability

YouTube, SoundCloud, and any other provider can change behavior, block access, rate-limit clients, or change their terms. Spice may stop resolving or downloading provider content without warning.

## No Warranty

The app is provided as-is, without warranty. Playback, downloads, sync, invites, member management, and resolver behavior are best-effort.
